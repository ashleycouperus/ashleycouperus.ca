  • Excel Woodwork Rebrand

  • Objective

    Excel Woodwork decided to rebrand their company to showcase a professional image.

  • Challenges

    The challenge was creating a logo that would reflect their company and values without being stereotypical.

  • My Role

    I was in charge of the overall design, production, logo, branding guide, stationary and deliverables.

Original Logo

Below is the original logo of Excel Woodwork.

Research

Before I started to sketch out logo designs that popped into my head, I had to spend time researching. I needed to determine the competitors of Excel Woodwork, target audience, geographic, etc.

Sketches

After gathering all the necessary information I needed in order to produce a unique logo that would work for their business, I began sketching out many logo ideas. The process started by sketching any ideas that came to mind and then choosing the best options. I then took the strongest ideas and produced many more variation of those, and followed these steps until I was able to produce the strongest logo design.

Excel Woodwork Logo

The Excel Woodwork logo reflects the client needs by establishing a logo design that represents Excel Woodwork’s services and values. The combination of the saw and hammer generates a strong contrast between positive and negative space which captures the attention of the target audience. The logo represents the tools and time that Excel Woodwork puts into each and every one of the products they manufacture. Rotation in the saw blade symbolizes Excel Woodwork’s continuous sales and impressive customer satisfaction.

Stationary Sketches

After determining the logo design, I began researching and sketching out possible layouts for stationary. By playing with the shape and elements from the logo, I was able to come up with a few solutions.

Stationary

The stationary includes an enveloppe, double sided business card, and letterhead. It was important to ensure all aspects of the stationary worked well together and look cohesive. This was achieved by using the saw blade in both the letterhead and enveloppe. The business card stands out from your average business card by die cutting the imagery of the logo.

Branding Guide

The very last step of rebranding Excel Woodwork was to provide them with a Branding Guideline. The guidline ensures that the brand will never get lost and the logo will always be used in the properly to accurately represent the brand.
