  • Excel Woodwork Rebrand

  • Objective

    Excel Woodwork rebranded their company in order to be perceived as a professional, knowledgeable woodworking company.

  • Challenges

    The challenge was creating a logo that would reflect their company and values without being stereotypical.

  • My Role

    I was in charge of the overall design, production, logo, branding guide, stationary and deliverables.

Original Logo

Below is the original logo of Excel Woodwork.

Research

Before starting to sketch logo designs, it is important to spend time researching. It’s crucial to gather all necessary information to fully understand the company’s competitors, target audience, geographic, etc.

Sketches

In order to produce a unique logo that would suit Excel Woodwork, pencil hit the paper to sketch out as many logo ideas possible. The process started by sketching any ideas that came to mind, then choosing the best options. The process continued by taking the strongest ideas and producing more variations. These steps were repeated until the strongest logo design was discovered.

Excel Woodwork Logo

The Excel Woodwork logo reflects the client needs by establishing a logo design that represents Excel Woodwork’s services and values. The combination of the saw and hammer generates a strong contrast between positive and negative space which captures the attention of the target audience. The logo represents the tools and time that Excel Woodwork puts into each and every one of the products they manufacture. Rotation in the saw blade symbolizes Excel Woodwork’s continuous sales and impressive customer satisfaction.

    • Stationary Sketches

    • After determining the logo design, it was time to start researching and sketching out possible stationary layouts. It was easy to use shapes and elements from the logo to come up with a few solutions.

    Stationary

    • The stationary includes an envelope, double sided business card, and letterhead. It was important to ensure all aspects of the stationary we’re cohesive. This was achieved by using the saw blade in both the letterhead and envelope. The business card stands out from your average business card by die cutting the imagery of the logo.

    Branding Guide

    • The last step of rebranding Excel Woodwork was to provide them with a Branding Guideline. The guideline ensures that the brand will never get lost across media/print and the logo will always be used properly to accurately represent the brand.
